Title: Examining the Solow Growth Model: An Empirical Analysis of Economic Growth in Australia

Author: Cen Sun

Solow Growth Model Australia Economic Growth Empirical Analysis Capital

1. Introduction

This paper explores the suitability of the Solow Growth Model in interpreting Australia's economic growth over the past 46 years, with a specific emphasis on total factor productivity (TFP). By leveraging data from April 1978 to January 2024, this research conducts an empirical analysis to estimate TFP and evaluate its relationship with Australia's economic conditions. The results indicate that the Solow Growth Model effectively explains the real-life economic growth in Australia, thus confirming the applicability of established fundamental theories of economic growth in this rapidly developing world. This also highlights the significance of technological progress and efficiency enhancements in fostering economic development (the factors of TFP).

2. Purpose and Scope

  1. The paper examines the suitability of the Solow Growth Model in interpreting Australia's economic growth over 46 years, with an emphasis on TFP.

The paper provides a comprehensive analysis of how the Solow Growth Model can be applied to understand economic growth in Australia, with a particular emphasis on the role of TFP.

3. Background

The paper explores the applicability of the Solow Growth Model in interpreting Australia's economic growth over the past 46 years, with a specific focus on Total Factor Productivity (TFP). Australia's economy has shown resilience and adaptability, transitioning from reliance on agriculture and resources to a more diverse economy based on services and innovation. The country's participation in international trade agreements and foreign investments has ensured strong and stable connections with other economies, making it an ideal case study for examining the relationship between TFP and economic growth within the Solow Growth Model framework.

4. Analysis

The empirical analysis conducted in the paper uses data from April 1978 to January 2024 to estimate TFP and evaluate its relationship with Australia's economic conditions. The Solow Growth Model is applied, considering variables such as output (Y), investment (I), labor (N), capital (K), and consumption (C). The model incorporates the Cobb-Douglas Function to represent the production function, with TFP as a variable. Linear regression analysis is used to estimate TFP and test its correlation with Australia's economic patterns. The paper also examines specific events like the 1978 agricultural growth, the 2008 financial crisis, the 2011 floods, and the 2020 COVID-19 pandemic to understand their impact on TFP and economic growth.

5. Conclusion

The findings of the paper suggest that the Solow Growth Model effectively explains Australia's economic growth, confirming the applicability of established fundamental theories of economic growth. The model's predictions align with the country's economic trajectory, emphasizing the importance of technological progress and efficiency improvements in driving economic growth. The paper acknowledges its limitations, such as using a simplified Solow Growth Model and examining only a few significant events over the past 46 years, and suggests potential areas for future research, including a more in-depth analysis and a larger sample size to enhance the accuracy of the model's fit.
