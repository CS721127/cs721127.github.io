Reflections on the Economics of Life:
What I Learned from My Economics Class

Posted Jun 24, 2024· By Cen· Academic Ideas

1. General Thinking

If someone asks me what I learned the least from my econ class, I will say, Economics; if they continue to ask, what about the most? I will say, economics. What I really learned from econ class is not what is called "Economics" as a subject that teaches how to do calculus and such maths stuff, but the economics as a mindset when dealing with different problems — the ways of thinking.

It is true that Economics does have a significant effect on our daily money-based life. However, as ordinary people, very few of us would eventually have an opportunity to participate in those macro decisions. What is the real meaning for the majority who will not take econ-related jobs? Are they studying economics just for the 5 on the AP exam? I believe most people do, and start advertising the subject as an easy thing to take for an assured 5, and a representation of money. But, how many of them have ever thought about why we are developing economics, and what economics is.

I used to be that kind of person, seeking it as a job opportunity for earning assured money. But that's what I learned from the class: Always ask why, and how. Economics studies how people make choices and how to achieve maximum social utility.

2. Focus on Economics

As in the basic economics idea, people would be able to learn about supply and demand, where the intersection decides what the prices are, as well as the quantity. These values initially provide a standard for people to judge their decisions easily but now have become the only standard that decides decisions, known as "profit."

Never overestimate the importance of a quantitative value — the world is so complex that no one can fully control it. All the numbers we calculate can only give us a semblance of evidence that this might be the best way; but the other factors that are ignored may change the final outcome.

Since categorical data is always hard to measure, doing a quantization job for the other side makes your value clearer. It can easily give people a brief sense of whether they should cooperate with you, hire you, or reject you, since the quantitative value will give the first thought. As exemplified by Alexandre Dumas' application for the congressman position, he used this strategy and succeeded.

However, as we focus on the value of numbers, money is taking a dominant status in people's minds. As Mr. Rush often says, "Ask why" — for what reasons was money designed, and for what purpose? Money is like a contract, a symbol of a product; it is not, as many people think, valuable in itself — it is meaningless without being issued by the government.

If and only if money has been transformed into a real good that can be touched and used does it have its value; otherwise, it is just a piece of paper. This reminds us in our daily spending that, as long as we can meet our basic "needs," we can spend our money to purchase things at the time they should be spent; but in other cases where it is meaningless to spend, we need to know how to save as well.

Understanding why opportunity cost exists and how it works could also make our lives better. By underlining the idea of cost and benefit, and setting a sense of cost and benefit when thinking about questions, it will help us make choices easier. As a rational person (an assumption of basic economics), by following the cost and benefit, and thinking about the drawbacks of choosing different options, a tough choice question would be solved more easily.

In this case, if people are irrational (which is true in most cases in reality), all the results we have actually make no sense. Emotions in people's minds, the idea of revenge, the evolutionary focus as humans, etc., will always change the result, making a seemingly trustworthy idea like Basic Game Theory useless. The complexity of humans makes any assumption or study of any subject that studies humans hard to work. However, when we remain skeptical and understand how it comes about when studying it, we will be able to find it and consider those, finding the right answer by ourselves.

3. Additional Thoughts

In addition, these are some of my personal thoughts. Economics' main purpose is for overall welfare, but more and more people nowadays are profit-oriented, which is also a drawback due to the misunderstanding of economic concepts. By chance, I got to know Buddhist Economics, which focuses never on profit or personal benefit, but on overall welfare. As I understand, it seems impossible to fully achieve, but I want to try it, wish to have a deeper understanding of economics, and find the function of what people always say, making it at least a bit progressive; and would be able to maximize the bliss that could possibly exist in society.
