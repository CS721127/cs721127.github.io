Jixi:
A Culinary Journey Through Time and Culture

Posted Oct 30, 2024· By Cen· Culture Share

Jixi, located on the banks of Xingkai (Khanka) Lake and the Wusuli (Usuri) River in Heilongjiang Province, China, is one of the eastern border cities. Its jurisdictional area is somewhat equivalent to (NYC + Ville de Paris + London + 서울 + とうきょう) multiplied by five.

The Snow Scene of 201 Road, Jixi

Jixi is a mountainous city, but unlike Chongqing, which truly builds its city on the mountains, Jixi is mainly built on slopes. Unlike Chongqing's visually stunning and magical effects, Jixi simply sits amidst the undulations.

The Slope of Jixi's Main Road

As the site of the famous Sino-Soviet Border War, Jixi was where it took place and has played a significant role in the border frictions between China and the Soviet Union since ancient times.

The Dongfanghong Wetland Park in Jixi

Moreover, Jixi is a forgotten resource city, known as the "Coal City" and the "Graphite City," which played an important role in the early stages of China's heavy industry construction and is now gradually transforming into a modern new city, shedding its heavy industrial facade.

The Snow World Held in Shuangya Mountain

Every winter, Jixi begins its famous Ice and Snow World, where ice is used to create a variety of different sculptures, full of life and vigor. Although it doesn't receive as much attention as Harbin, which has recently gained popularity and is just an hour's drive away from Jixi, the quality of the ice sculptures in Jixi's Ice and Snow World has not diminished. In the hands of ice sculpture artists, they bring to life dragons, tigers, castles, and various other shapes.

A Food City Like No Other

Jixi is also a little-known but vibrant food city. In Mishan, one of the county-level cities under the jurisdiction of Jixi, there is the largest border lake in Asia — Xingkai (Khanka) Lake, which borders Russia across the sea. The Xingkai Lake White Fish Banquet, featuring the original white fish of Xingkai Lake, is a celebration of the region's natural resources.

The Xingkai Lake

Another county-level city, Hulin (Tiger Forest), has the Wusuli (Ussuri) River, which also serves as a border dividing line between China and Russia. The "Wusuli Boat Song," adapted from the Hezhen folk tune, has become widely known throughout the country.

乌苏里江来长又长
蓝蓝的江水起波浪
赫哲人撒开千张网
船儿满江鱼满仓 The Ussuri River is long and vast, / With blue waves rolling wide and fast. / The Hezhen people cast a thousand nets, / Boats fill the river, fish fill the nets. — Wusuli Boat Song

A Melting Pot of Flavors

Jixi's culinary culture is like a melting pot, where the flavors of the Manchu, Korean, Shandong, Russian, and Northeast China interweave to create a unique culinary experience. The city's cuisine is a testament to its historical and cultural diversity, reflecting its rich past and present.

Jixi spicy dishes, although similar in appearance to Korean kimchi, have a very special taste: fragrant but not spicy. Dried chili oil mixed with unique braised beef, chrysanthemum vegetables, tofu puffs, and accompanied by onions and cilantro — a plate of dishes can eat several bowls of rice. This is also one of my favorite foods.

Jixi's Spicy Dishes called "Lacai"

As a city close to Russia, the rich Russian cuisine has also blown here. Sauerkraut stew and other dishes are our demand for food in the long and cold winter. Borscht, black bread, etc., have also become our daily dishes.

Due to historical factors, many Shandong people came to the Northeast in the 19th and 20th centuries to "venture into the pass," bringing the unique Shandong cuisine. Among them, classic dishes such as Shandong buns and scallion-braised sea cucumber have also begun to spread in Jixi.

The Scallion-Braised Sea Cucumber

Jixi's most famous dish, "Jixi cold noodles," is modified from the Korean cold noodles. These noodles are made from buckwheat or wheat noodles, with a taste that is heavier on sour, sweet, and salty flavors. Gradually spreading from Jixi, even in Beijing, you can also see their presence.

The Famous Jixi Cold Noodle

Jixi's culinary heritage and diverse range of foods make it a city that truly embraces the flavors of the past and present. There, food is not only a necessity but also a celebration of culture and history, leaving a deep impression on all visitors.

If you have the opportunity, I would highly recommend you to visit there, and I guarantee you will gain a different experience!
